ERROR LIST
 
-Misapplying derivative/integral identity: Student differentiates or integrates (1+x)^n to evaluate ∑r·ⁿCᵣ or ∑ⁿCᵣ/(r+1) but substitutes the wrong value of x, forgets the integration constant, or fails to adjust the limits, yielding a wrong closed-form.

-Confusing 'sum of coefficients' with 'sum of terms': Student conflates the sum of binomial coefficients (obtained by setting x=1) with the sum of the terms of the expansion for general x, leading to incorrect values when the question asks for the sum of coefficients of a specific subset of terms.

-Sign error in alternating-sign expansions: When expanding (a - b)^n, student forgets that (-1)^r attaches to each term, especially in products like (1+x)^p(1-x)^q where signs from both factors must be tracked simultaneously.

-Multinomial partition omission: When finding the coefficient of x^k in (1+x+x²)^n or similar trinomials, student misses valid partitions of k into sums of 0s, 1s, and 2s across n factors, undercounting the contributing terms.

-Fractional exponent sign/direction error: When the general term has the form x^{f(r)} with f(r) = an - br for fractional a,b, the student incorrectly sets up the exponent equation (e.g., swapping the sign or mixing up which base contributes which exponent), yielding a wrong term index.

-Counting integral terms with wrong LCM: When counting rational/integral terms in (a^{1/p} + b^{1/q})^n, student computes the periodicity as p·q instead of lcm(p,q), or miscounts the endpoints, getting the wrong number of valid term indices.

-Misidentifying the modular base: In remainder problems, student writes a^n mod m but decomposes a as (m+k) when k is not the correct residue (e.g., writing 7^{103} mod 23 as (23-16)^{103} instead of recognizing 7³ = 343 = 14·23 + 21 and working with the cyclic order of 7 mod 23).

-Incomplete simplification before expansion: Student attempts to expand a compound expression like ((x+1)/(x^{2/3}-x^{1/3}+1) - (x-1)/(x-x^{1/2}))^{10} without first simplifying each fraction via algebraic identities (a³+1 = (a+1)(a²-a+1), etc.), leading to an intractable expansion.

-Off-by-one in general term indexing: Student writes the (r+1)th term as ⁿCᵣ a^r b^{n-r} instead of ⁿCᵣ a^{n-r} b^r, or confuses whether T_r means the rth or (r+1)th term, leading to an incorrect exponent equation.

-Dropping the 'term from the end' reversal: Student treats the kth term from the end as T_k from the beginning instead of T_{n-k+2}, leading to a wrong ratio in beginning-to-end term ratio problems.
