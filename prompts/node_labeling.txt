TOPIC: Matrices And Determinants
 
---
 
ARCHETYPE LIST
 
-Adjoint-Determinant Chain Unwinding: The student must recursively apply the identity |adj(A)| = |A|^(n-1) through nested compositions of adj, scalar multiples, and powers, tracking exponent arithmetic across multiple layers without losing track of the base.

-System-of-Equations Parameter Classification: The student must determine the value(s) of parameters (λ, μ, α, β) that cause a system of linear equations to have infinitely many solutions, no solution, or a unique solution, typically by computing the determinant of the coefficient matrix and then checking consistency of the augmented matrix.

-Matrix Power Pattern Recognition: The student must compute a high power of a matrix (often Aⁿ for large n) by discovering a recurrence, periodicity, or a closed-form structure such as A² = I, A² = kA, or a nilpotent decomposition.

-Determinant-as-Function Evaluation: The student must treat a determinant with variable entries (often trigonometric or polynomial) as a function, expand it using row/column operations to simplify into a recognizable expression, then optimize or evaluate at specific points.

-Counting Matrices Under Constraints: The student must enumerate matrices from a finite element set satisfying a constraint (e.g., sum of diagonal elements of AᵀA equals a constant, A = A⁻¹, det(A) = 0), converting the matrix constraint into a combinatorial counting problem.

-Cayley-Hamilton Inversion and Polynomial Identity: The student must use the Cayley-Hamilton theorem (or derive it from the characteristic equation) to express A⁻¹ or high powers of A as a linear combination of lower powers, then substitute into a given relation to extract unknowns.

-Similarity Transform and Conjugation Tracking: The student must track a matrix through a sequence of similarity transformations (P⁻¹AP, PAPᵀ) recognizing that determinant and trace are invariant, or must reconstruct A from its action on given vectors.

-Eigenvalue Extraction from Matrix Equations: The student must extract eigenvalue information from a given matrix equation (characteristic equation, matrix polynomial identity, or eigenvector conditions) without being told to think in terms of eigenvalues.

-Skew-Symmetric / Symmetric Structure Exploitation: The student must recognize that a matrix is skew-symmetric (Aᵀ = -A) or symmetric (Aᵀ = A) and apply consequences (odd-order skew-symmetric has det 0, orthogonal matrix properties, decomposition into symmetric + skew-symmetric parts) to shortcut the computation.

-Determinant Row/Column Operation Simplification: The student must apply strategic row or column operations to a determinant with polynomial, factorial, or structured entries to collapse it into a computable form, often requiring recognition of a hidden pattern such as common factors or telescoping differences.

-Matrix Reconstruction from Action on Vectors: The student must reconstruct a matrix (or compute its determinant/trace) from its action on specific input vectors, effectively solving a system of equations for matrix entries or recognizing column relationships.

-Homogeneous System Nontriviality Detection: The student must determine when a homogeneous system has non-trivial solutions by setting the coefficient determinant to zero, often with trigonometric or parametric entries, requiring the student to evaluate the determinant symbolically first.
